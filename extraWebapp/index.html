Enabling integrative modeling of human immunological data

The Human Immunology Project Consortium (HIPC) program, established in 2010 by the NIAID Division of Allergy, Immunology, and Transplantation, is a major collaborative effort that is generating large amounts of cross-center and cross-assay data — including high-dimensional data — to characterize the status of the immune system in diverse populations under both normal conditions and in response to stimuli. This large data problem has given birth to ImmuneSpace, a powerful data management and analysis engine where datasets can be easily explored and analyzed using state-of-the-art computational tools.

Access to the portal is currently available by invitation only.
